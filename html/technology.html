The stars of the show !

Discover... The SLS

A super heavy-lift rocket that’s able to propel tons of material fast enough to deliver them at 384 400km ! It was purposefully designed for deep space missions and is the backbone of the mission as it already sent the Orion module on the 16th of November 2022 and will continue to carry out the other phases of the mission. The actual propulsion of the core stage is made possible thanks to the four RS-25 engines powered by liquid hydrogen and liquid oxygen, operating with a power of 71 140 horse power each. Those are the result of years of studies on high-pressure engines, started in the 1960s and applied first to the rocket Saturn V.

Then the... Orion module

A space capsule is an often-crewed spacecraft that uses a blunt-body reentry capsule to reenter the Earth's atmosphere without wings. Our capsule is where you'll spend your time during the flight. It includes a space gym, cinema, and plenty of other activities to keep you entertained.

The terminology... Gateway - Ihab

Created by the ESA, it will be the habitation of the lunar Gateway, a huge structure that will orbit the Moon and serve as an international space station to live and work. It will mark the transition between the ISS to more performing space habitats. The challenges are to build a lighter structure with an optimized thermal control system.

Next one... Esprit

Esprit is a pretty long acronym for ‘European System Providing Refuelling, Infrastructure and Telecommunications’. It will ensure that the communication between the Moon and the Gateway happens correctly. Its second goal is to refuel the Gateway with xenon and chemical propellants.

Last one... HLS

We obviously need a way to connect the Gateway and the lunar surface, and it is what’s done thanks to the Human Landing System. An actual lunar bus to transport the astronauts on a land full of surprises ! Because of the technical difficulties of conducting such an operation, NASA partners with five companies to adapt the module to the needs of frequent lunar surface missions ; Blue Origin, Dynetics, Lockheed Martin, Northrop Grumman and SpaceX.
