Discover the mission

Worldwide project

The Artemis program which is led the United Statesâ€™ space agency was established in 2017 and aims at bringing back humans on the Moon. The mission as a whole is currently divided into six main parts and they will be accomplished with the help of various worldwide space agencies; mainly the ESA (Europe), JAXA (Japan) and CSA (Canada).

Challenges

Getting a foothold on the Moon is definitely not going to be a piece of cake as the lunar environment is far from being welcoming for human life. There are many challenges to be taken into account, the main ones being the absence of atmosphere, the major temperature swings, the razor-sharp dust and finding a source of water. Having to face those inhospitable conditions will help foster development of new technologies as well as making engineers the happiest people on Earth.

Main phases

The first phase of the mission started last year with Artemis I which was a test flight that was reaching for the orbit of the Moon. Then Artemis II will be similar to the latter but as a crewed flight, and the following one will be paramount in the whole mission as it is when the team of astronauts is going to be walking on the lunar surface. Artemis 4 to 6 will be focusing on delivering the different part of a lunar space station (Gateway), as well as starting to make use of it as a research station, in the way that astronauts could go back and forth between the orbiting structure and the Moon.
